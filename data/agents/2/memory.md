## SECTION 2: TOP 10 VENUE TYPES IN WYOMING — RANKED BY OPPORTUNITY

Wyoming is unique: low population density, high blue-collar workforce, heavy highway traffic, extreme weather keeping people indoors, and a strong bar culture. Here's where your machines will perform best:

**Why:** Wyoming sits on three major interstate corridors. Tens of thousands of long-haul truckers pass through weekly. Truckers are heavy nicotine users, they travel with cash or card, and they're away from convenience stores for hours at a time. A machine at a TA, Pilot, Love's, or independent truck stop captures captive, high-purchase-intent customers 24/7.

**Best markets:** Cheyenne (I-80/I-25 junction), Rawlins, Rock Springs, Laramie, Evanston, Gillette, Buffalo (I-90)

**Revenue potential:** $1,200 – $2,500/month per machine

**Why:** This is your cleanest placement legally — the venue is already 21+, so no age verification burden on the machine itself. Customers are drinking, socializing, and highly likely to impulse-purchase a vape. They also frequently forget their own vapes. Bar patrons are your ideal customer.

**Best markets:** Cheyenne (downtown), Casper, Laramie (college town), Jackson, Gillette, Rock Springs, Sheridan

**Revenue potential:** $800 – $3,000/month depending on foot traffic

Bring a one-page flyer showing:
1. Photo of the machine
2. "Here's what venues like yours typically earn: $X–$X per month"

**The Trial Close is powerful.** Nobody feels locked in. Very few say no to a 30-day trial.

Use DocuSign or just a physical signature. Do NOT skip the agreement — protects you if a venue owner changes or tries to claim ownership of the machine.

## SECTION 4: REVENUE OPTIMIZATION — INCREASE REVENUE PER MACHINE

### Current Baseline (Estimated)
Average vape vending machine: **$600–$1,000/month gross**
After product cost (COGS ~45%): **~$330–$550 gross profit**
After venue commission (20%): **~$265–$440 net**

### Levers to Pull

---

**Lever 1: Product Mix Optimization (Biggest Impact, Fastest)**
Most operators stock what's easy to get, not what sells. The fix:
- Track sales by SKU at each machine
- After 60 days, cut the bottom 20% of SKUs (slow movers tie up capital and take up slots)
- Add the top-selling disposable brands nationally: Elf Bar (Lost Mary), Geek Bar, Breeze, SMOK
- Stock mid-range price points ($15-$22/unit) as your bread-and-butter — avoid going too cheap (kills margin) or too expensive (kills velocity)
- Add a premium SKU at $28-$35 — even if it sells 3x/month, pure margin

**Expected revenue lift: 15–30%**

---

**Lever 2: Price Anchoring**
If you're selling at $15-$18, test $18-$22. Vending machine buyers are impulse buyers — price elasticity is low in this context. They drove to the bar, they're having a good time, they want a vape *right now*. An extra $3-$4 rarely kills the sale.

**Expected revenue lift: 10–20% with minimal volume loss**

---

**Lever 3: Machine Placement Within Venue**
Where in the bar/venue the machine sits matters enormously:
- **Best:** Near the bathroom hallway, near the bar itself, or near the exit (all high-traffic, high-dwell moments)
- **Worst:** Back corner, behind a pillar, near the kitchen

Have a frank conversation with venue owners: "I want to make sure this earns you the most — can we try it near [spot] for a month?"

**Expected revenue lift: 20–50% just from moving a machine**

---

**Lever 4: Machine Lighting & Visibility**
Make sure the machine is lit, clean, and has fresh product visible. A machine that looks full and well-stocked drives more impulse purchases than one that looks half-empty and dingy. Clean the front panel every visit.

---

**Lever 5: Add Complementary SKUs**
Beyond traditional vapes, consider adding:
- Nicotine pouches (Zyn, On!, Rogue) — legal everywhere, no age machine complexity in most states
- CBD disposables (higher margin, different customer)
- Lighters / accessories as a filler SKU

Nicotine pouches especially are surging — they're the fastest-growing nicotine category in the U.S.

**Expected revenue lift: 10–20% from new category**

---

**Lever 6: Negotiate Better Vendor Pricing**
At 10+ machines, you have negotiating power. Contact distributors directly (not just your local vape shop supplier):
- Midwest Goods (wholesale vape distributor)
- Empire Distribution
- Direct from brands for volume accounts

Target COGS below 40% (currently industry average is 42-48%). Every 5% improvement in COGS on $10k/month gross revenue = $500/month straight to your bottom line.

### Route Optimization
- Group machines by geography into **routes** (all Cheyenne machines one day, all Casper machines another)
- Restock on a fixed schedule (every 10-14 days minimum, or trigger-based if you have telemetry)
- Never restock more than 50% full — time wasted
- Never let a machine go below 30% — lost sales
- **Target:** 2 hours per route day, including drive time

---

### The Stocking Kit
Keep a rolling stock of your top 20 SKUs in your vehicle. Use a labeled plastic bin system sorted by SKU. Replenish your vehicle inventory weekly from your storage unit/home base. Never make special trips to a machine for a single SKU.

---

### The Monthly Revenue Cadence
1. **1st of month:** Pull sales report from each machine (telemetry or manual count)
2. **2nd-3rd:** Calculate venue commissions, pay them out
3. **4th-5th:** Reorder product for the next 30 days
4. **Weekly:** Review low-stock alerts, plan next restock route

---

### When to Hire (Milestone-Based)
- **0-5 machines:** You do everything
- **6-10 machines:** Hire a part-time route driver ($15-18/hr, 8-10 hrs/week) — this buys you back 8+ hours/week
- **11-20 machines:** Route driver becomes 20-25 hrs/week; you focus only on new placements and vendor relationships
- **20+ machines:** Hire an operations manager, step fully into CEO role

**Key principle:** Systematize the route before you hire. Document every step in a simple Google Doc so the driver can run it without calling you.

---

### Protecting Your Placements (Retention)
The biggest ops risk is losing a placement you've spent time securing. Protect them:
- Visit each venue in person at least **once per month** beyond restocking — even just to say hi to the manager
- Send a monthly text: "Hey [Name], here's your commission for March — $[X]. Machine's running great. Let me know if anything comes up."
- Know the owner AND the bartenders/staff — staff turnover kills placements when only one person knows who you are
- If a venue changes management, introduce yourself to the new GM immediately
